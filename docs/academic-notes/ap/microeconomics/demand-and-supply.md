## Formula of Price Elasticity of Demand

$PED = \dfrac{\Delta Quantity\ Demanded\%}{\Delta Price\%}$

$PED \in [0, +\infty)$

### Perfectly Elastic Demand

$PED = \infty$, demand curve is a horizontal line.

### Relatively Elastic Demand

$PED > 1$, $\Delta QD\% > \Delta P\%$.

### Unit Elastic Demand

$PED = 1$, $\Delta QD\% = \Delta P\%$.

### Relatively Inelastic Demand

$0 < PED < 1$, $\Delta QD\% < \Delta P\%$.

### Perfectly Inelastic Demand

$PED = 0$, demand curve is a vertical line.

## Total Revenue

$TR = P \cdot Q$

$Price \propto TR \implies Inelastic$

$Price \propto \dfrac{1}{TR} \implies Elastic$

$Price = C \implies Unit\ Elastic$

## Formula of Price Elasticity of Supply

$PES = \dfrac{\Delta Quantity\ Supplied\%}{\Delta Price\%}$

$PES \in [0, +\infty)$

**Simple formula**:

- $\Delta Quantity\ Supplied\% = \dfrac{Q_2 - Q_1}{Q_1}$
- $\Delta Price\% = \dfrac{P_2-P_1}{P_1}$

**Mid-Point formula**:

- $\Delta Quantity\ Supplied\% = \dfrac{Q_2 - Q_1}{\frac{Q_2+Q_1}2}$
- $\Delta Price\% = \dfrac{P_2-P_1}{\frac{P_2+P_1}2}$

### Perfectly Elastic Supply

$PES = \infty$, supply curve is a horizontal line.

### Relatively Elastic Supply

$PES > 1$, $\Delta QS\% > \Delta P\%$.

### Unit Elastic Supply

$PES = 1$, $\Delta QS\% = \Delta P\%$.

### Relatively Inelastic Supply

$0 < PES < 1$, $\Delta QS\% < \Delta P\%$.

### Perfectly Inelastic Supply

$PES = 0$, supply curve is a vertical line.

## Income Elasticity of Demand

$YED = \dfrac{\Delta Quantity\%}{\Delta Income\%}$

$YED > 0 \implies Normal\ good$

$YES < 0 \implies Inferior\ good$

## Cross-Price Elasticity of Demand

$XED = \dfrac{\Delta Quantity\%\ of\ X}{\Delta Price\%\ of\ Y}$

$XED > 0 \implies Substitutes$

$XED < 0 \implies Complement$

## Equilibrium Price

The intersect of **Demand Curve** and **Supply Curve**.
